How to Use

This prompt is optimized for creating a distinct contrast between a sharp, still subject and a blurred, moving environment. For the best results in Gemini, upload a reference photo with a neutral expression and clear facial features. The AI will apply the motion blur to the background and foreground elements while keeping your face locked in sharp focus. To enhance the subway authenticity, you can add a specific city name or train line color, such as `interior of a New York City 7 train` or `London Underground carriage,` which helps the AI select the correct interior architecture and pole placement. If the motion blur on the passengers looks unnatural or like ghosting, ensure you are using a model version or setting that supports motion rendering well. You can adjust the intensity of the stillness contrast by modifying `surrounded by moving passengers` to `surrounded by a few stationary commuters and some blurred passersby` if you want a slightly less chaotic frame. The negative prompt is crucial here to prevent the AI from defaulting to a clean, sterile portrait mode; keep those terms included in your generation settings.

FAQ

  • The motion blur on the passengers looks like a mistake or a glitch. How can I make it look more natural?
    Motion blur in AI generation can sometimes be interpreted as 'ghosting' rather than smooth streaks. To improve the natural look, you should specify the direction of the movement relative to the camera. Add directional cues like `passengers moving left to right across the frame with natural motion blur` or `streaking horizontally due to train movement.` This gives the AI a vector to apply the blur along. Additionally, reducing the strength descriptor from `heavy motion blur` to `subtle directional blur` can sometimes yield a more photorealistic result, as extreme blur is hard for AI to render without artifacts. You can also emphasize the shutter speed in the camera specs: `shot with a slow shutter speed of 1/15th to capture motion trails.`
  • The vertical poles in the foreground are covering my face. How do I fix the framing?
    This is a common issue with the 'framed through' instruction. The AI sometimes treats the foreground element as a prominent subject rather than a framing device. To fix this, add spatial context. Change `framed through vertical poles` to `vertical poles blur softly at the extreme left and right edges of the frame, not obstructing the center.` This explicitly tells the AI to keep the middle of the composition clear for your face. You can also add `the subject's face is fully visible and unobstructed by foreground elements.` This ensures the poles act as a vignette on the periphery rather than a mask over the subject.
  • I love the yellow-green subway light, but it makes my teeth and eyes look strange. Can I neutralize that?
    Yes, the strong fluorescent color cast can sometimes pick up on the white balance of teeth and the sclera of the eyes in an unflattering way. To counteract this while keeping the environmental mood, add a specific white balance correction to the prompt: `Despite the warm yellow-green ambient light, teeth and eye whites appear natural and neutral.` Alternatively, you can change the light source description slightly to a more universally flattering tone, like `warm vintage incandescent subway light` instead of `yellow-green fluorescent.` The key is maintaining the 'old subway' vibe while pulling back the specific green spike that affects skin and teeth tones.
