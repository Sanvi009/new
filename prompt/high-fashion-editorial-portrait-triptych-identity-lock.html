Three distinct poses: hand near mouth, head tilt with hand on forehead, ring near lips. Luxury fashion editorial, cinematic realism, 9:16 aspect ratio.

Prompt Explanation

This prompt is a masterclass in precision engineering for AI image generation, designed to produce a complex, multi-frame editorial portrait that feels like a spread from a luxury fashion magazine. Its creative purpose is twofold: to showcase the subject in a series of intimate, posed vignettes that tell a cohesive story, and to do so while achieving an unprecedented level of identity preservation, or 'Identity Lock.' The triptych format itself is a bold creative choice, allowing for a narrative to unfold across three distinct yet connected images—a close-up, a mid-shot, and an intimate detail shot—all within a single, vertically stacked composition. This structure immediately elevates the portrait from a simple headshot to a piece of conceptual art. The prompt_text is an exhaustive and meticulously detailed instruction set that leaves virtually nothing to chance. It begins with a fortress-like declaration of identity preservation, specifying not just that the face should be the same, but enumerating the exact facial landmarks to be protected: 'identical facial structure, jawline, nose shape, eye spacing, lips, skin texture.' This level of granularity is crucial because it forces the AI to consider each of these components individually, rather than attempting to generate a generic 'handsome' face. It then explicitly forbids common AI alterations with a powerful negative prompt embedded within the main text, targeting 'face swapping,' 'beautification,' and 'CGI look.' The prompt then meticulously builds the scene: the hair is described as 'black hair, neatly swept back,' the outfit as a 'white dress shirt layered with a tailored black vest,' and the accessories as a 'statement ring' and 'subtle line-art tattoo.' This specificity ensures a consistent and recognizable look across all three frames. The true genius of the prompt lies in how it structures the triptych. It doesn't just say 'three frames'; it describes each one in detail. The top frame is a 'close-up portrait with my hand near my mouth, direct eye contact, composed and confident expression.' The middle frame is a 'head tilted sideways hand resting across the forehead, elegant and editorial pose.' The bottom frame is an 'intimate close-up with the ring near my lips, eyes cast downward, introspective mood.' By providing these distinct yet thematically linked descriptions, the prompt guides the AI to generate three different poses that flow naturally from one to the next, creating a visual narrative. The lighting and background are kept consistent across all frames with instructions for 'soft directional studio lighting, neutral-to-cool tones' and a 'plain muted grey studio backdrop,' ensuring the triptych feels like a single, coherent photo shoot. The inclusion of a formal negative prompt at the end further reinforces what the AI must avoid, such as 'different face,' 'exaggerated jaw,' and 'extra accessories,' acting as a final filter for quality control. The importance of proportion, lighting, and realism in this prompt is paramount. The consistent 'soft directional studio lighting' is essential for sculpting the face in a flattering and realistic way across all three frames, ensuring the subject is recognizable in each. The mention of 'shallow depth of field' helps to focus attention on the subject and the key details, like the hand or the ring, blurring out the already minimal background. The 'realism level' is pushed by demanding 'cinematic realism,' 'ultra-detailed,' and 'realistic pores and details,' guiding the AI away from smooth, artificial textures and towards a photographic authenticity. The styling, from the 'fitted silhouette' of the vest to the 'statement ring,' is carefully chosen to project a sense of luxury and editorial sophistication. Elements that must not be changed are, first and foremost, the subject's identity. Any deviation from the reference image's facial features would break the core promise of the prompt. The triptych format itself is also non-negotiable; generating a single image or a different layout would not fulfill the creative brief. The consistent styling—the black swept-back hair, the white shirt and black vest, and the specific accessories—must remain to maintain coherence. However, variations are possible, as listed. The color of the vest could be changed from black to charcoal or navy, which would subtly alter the mood. The statement ring could be replaced with a different accessory, like cufflinks. The lighting could be shifted from neutral-cool to warmer sepia tones for a different feel. The poses themselves could be swapped for other editorial gestures, such as looking over a shoulder or adjusting a cuff, creating a new narrative within the same structural framework. Practical tips for achieving this are crucial. Using a high image weight (--iw 2.0 in Midjourney) is essential for the initial identity lock. However, as the instructions suggest, for perfect results, one might need to use a face-swapping tool like InsightFaceSwap as a refinement step. The negative prompt provided is a powerful tool and should be used verbatim to block unwanted alterations. A common mistake is to underestimate the AI's difficulty with the triptych format. Generating multiple times is often necessary to get a coherent layout. If the AI consistently fails to produce three clean, separate frames, a practical workaround is to generate each frame as a separate portrait using the same core descriptive elements and then composite them manually in photo editing software. This ensures perfect control over the composition. Another mistake is to be vague about the poses. By describing each frame's pose as specifically as 'hand near mouth' or 'ring near lips,' you give the AI clear, actionable visual targets, which is essential for the success of this complex and ambitious high-fashion editorial portrait triptych.

The Prompt 

A high-fashion editorial portrait triptych, composed of three horizontal frames stacked vertically, featuring the same person from the provided reference image (my face) with identical facial structure, jawline, nose shape, eye spacing, lips, skin texture, and overall identity fully preserved.
No facial alteration, no face swapping, no beautification beyond natural realism.
The subject has my exact facial features, natural skin tone, authentic expressions, and realistic pores and details maintaining my true identity.
Hair is styled black hair, neatly swept back (can be slightly textured), matching my natural hairline and volume.
Outfit: white dress shirt layered with a tailored black vest, clean and sharp, fitted silhouette.
Accessories: statement ring on the finger, subtle line-art tattoo on the hand, no additional jewelry.
Top frame: close-up portrait with my hand near my mouth, direct eye contact, composed and confident expression.
Middle frame: head tilted sideways hand resting across the forehead, elegant and editorial pose.
Bottom frame: intimate close-up with the ring near my lips, eyes cast downward, introspective mood.
Lighting: soft directional studio lighting, neutral-to-cool tones, smooth gradients, controlled shadows sculpting my face without distortion.
Background: plain muted grey studio backdrop, clean and minimal.
Style: luxury fashion magazine editorial, cinematic realism, shallow depth of field, ultra-detailed, professional photography Identity Lock / Face Accuracy Keywords
use my reference image, same person, same face, facial identity preserved, no face change, no new person, no gender change, realistic likeness, accurate facial proportions
Negative Prompt
different face, altered identity, beautified face, face swap, CGI look, anime, soft doll skin, smiling, exaggerated jaw, sharp cheekbones, text, watermark, logo, extra accessories. aspect ratio 9:16

How to Use This Prompt

1. Upload your high-resolution reference photo. 2. Use Midjourney with image prompt + full text, set --iw 2.0 for strong face adherence. 3. For perfect identity lock, generate first then refine with InsightFaceSwap. 4. Set aspect ratio to 9:16. 5. Use negative prompt as provided to block unwanted alterations. 6. Upscale for editorial print quality.

Why This Prompt Works

This prompt treats face preservation as the primary technical requirement. It names every facial landmark (jawline, nose, eye spacing) and explicitly forbids common AI pitfalls: face swap, beautification, gender change, and CGI smoothing. The triptych format is difficult but highly distinctive; by describing each frame's pose, outfit, and lighting consistently, the AI is forced to generate a coherent, usable multi-panel editorial image in one pass.

Prompt Variations

  • Change black vest to charcoal or navy
  • Replace ring with a different accessory: bracelet, cufflinks
  • Use warmer lighting and sepia grading
  • Add subtle smoke or shadow texture to background
  • Swap poses: looking over shoulder, adjusting cuff, holding glasses
  • Create a diptych (2 frames) instead of triptych

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What does 'Identity Lock' mean?
    Identity Lock is a keyword strategy that instructs the AI to preserve your exact facial features and not generate a new person. It works best when combined with a strong image weight and face-swap tools.
  • Why is there a negative prompt?
    The negative prompt blocks common AI 'improvements' like smooth skin, altered jawlines, or adding text/logos. It tells the model what NOT to do, which is essential for realistic identity preservation.
  • How do I get three stacked frames?
    The prompt explicitly describes a vertical stack of three horizontal images. Midjourney and DALL-E understand 'triptych' and 'stacked vertically'. If results are inconsistent, generate three separate portraits and composite them in Photoshop.
  • Can I use this for professional headshots?
    Yes. This prompt is designed for luxury fashion editorial and commercial personal branding. The neutral background and soft lighting make it suitable for agency submissions or LinkedIn.
  • What if the AI adds extra accessories?
    The negative prompt blocks 'extra accessories'. If the issue persists, add 'no earrings, no necklace, no bracelets' to your negative prompt.
