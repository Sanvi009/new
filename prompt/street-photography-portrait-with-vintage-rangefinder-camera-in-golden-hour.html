How to Use

This prompt is optimized for AI image generators that support reference image uploads for strict identity preservation, such as Gemini or Stable Diffusion with FaceID and IP-Adapter models. Begin by uploading a clear reference photo of yourself showing natural skin texture and facial structure without heavy filters. Paste the complete prompt text into the generation field exactly as written. The prompt includes a detailed negative prompt section to prevent common AI artifacts like plastic skin or cartoonish styling. To customize the look, you can adjust the wardrobe description. The beige trench coat and navy pinstripe suit create a specific high-end editorial look, but you can swap these for a leather jacket and dark jeans for a grittier street style or a casual blazer for a softer approach. The amber-tinted sunglasses can be changed to clear glasses, aviators, or removed entirely by deleting that line. If the camera prop obscures too much of the face, you can modify the prompt to 'holding the camera slightly lower revealing more of the eyes.' For best identity preservation, ensure your reference image is well-lit and front-facing. Use the 4:5 aspect ratio for a classic vertical portrait crop suitable for Instagram and editorial layouts.

FAQ

  • How do I make sure the AI doesn't add facial hair or change my face?
    This prompt includes a detailed negative prompt section specifically designed to prevent unwanted changes. The negative prompt explicitly lists 'facial hair, beard, mustache, goatee' and 'overly perfect skin, plastic skin, CGI look' among other restrictions. When using Gemini or Stable Diffusion, paste this negative prompt into the designated negative prompt field if available. If your AI tool does not have a separate negative prompt field, you can append it to the main prompt with '--no' before each term or use a weight of '-1' for those concepts. For identity preservation, the phrase 'exact same facial and physical identity from the uploaded image' is the primary driver. In Gemini, upload a clear reference photo. In Stable Diffusion, use a FaceID or IP-Adapter model with a weight between 0.7 and 0.9 to lock in your specific facial structure.
  • Can I change the sunglasses to clear glasses or remove them entirely?
    Yes, the prompt is fully customizable. The current description specifies 'amber-tinted rectangular sunglasses with thin metal frames.' To change this, simply replace that line with 'wears thin wire-rimmed clear optical glasses' or 'wears round tortoiseshell glasses.' To remove eyewear completely, delete the sunglasses line from the prompt. If you remove the sunglasses, you may want to slightly adjust the description of the eyes. For example, add 'eyes visible with slightly intense but playful gaze' to ensure the AI still captures the intended expression. The rest of the styling, lighting, and camera realism will remain consistent regardless of eyewear choices.
  • What if the camera ends up blocking my face too much in the final image?
    The prompt intentionally describes the subject 'holding a vintage rangefinder camera close to his face as if about to take a photo' to create that intimate, slightly intrusive street photography vibe. If the camera obscures too much of your face, you can modify the phrasing to 'holding the camera slightly lower near chin level, revealing the eyes and upper face clearly' or 'camera held at chest height angled up toward face.' You can also change the lens description from '35mm lens, close distance' to '50mm lens, slightly further back' which will naturally show more of the subject and less foreground camera distortion. Experiment with different camera positions while keeping the same candid mid-action feel described in the prompt.
