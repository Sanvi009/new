Paper Fragment Portrait Poster AI Prompt for Gemini

AI Image Prompts 2026-04-21 Brainloom

Generate a minimalist cinematic poster featuring an exact facial and physical identity reconstruction from an uploaded reference image, composed entirely of large square-cut paper fragments arranged in a slightly irregular 5x6 grid. The portrait spans from shoulders to the top of the head, with each paper tile containing a disjointed section of the face that collectively forms the complete likeness. The fragments appear three-dimensional with curled edges and lifted corners that cast tiny realistic shadows against a gunmetal concrete wall background visible through the gaps. Four of the facial paper squares contain subtle black handwritten text reading phrases like 'NEVER TELL ME ODDS' and 'REBEL HEART' directly on the skin image. Scattered irregularly around the outer perimeter of the central grid, green sticky notes with handwritten iconic quotes are attached to the wall at random positions. High-end studio lighting emphasizes the paper grain, ink texture, and the dimensional shadows of the tacked-down edges, creating a tactile mixed-media editorial look in a 4:5 aspect ratio.

The Prompt

A minimalist cinematic colored poster featuring my portrait with exact same facial and physical identity from the uplaoded image from shoulders to the top of the head. The face is reconstructed using a small number of large, square-cut paper fragments arranged in a simple grid [5x6 pieces]. Each paper fragment contains a disjointed part of the face which together reconstruct the portrait. The internal paper fragments constituting the face are not perfectly flat; many have slightly curled edges and lifted corners, casting tiny, realistic shadows that give them a three-dimensional, tacked-down appearance. 4 of these squares have subtle black handwritten text directly on the skin/image: "NEVER TELL ME ODDS", "SHOT FIRST", "REBEL HEART", "I KNOW" Scattered irregularly around the outer perimeter of this central grid in various randomly placed positions (not confined to the corners), several green sticky notes are attached to the gunmetal concrete wall, containing handwritten iconic quotes: "Never tell me the odds.", "You know, sometimes I amaze even myself.", "I've got a bad feeling about this.", "It's the ship that made the Kessel Run in less than 12 parsecs." The overall layout is slightly irregular with visible gaps between the pieces showing gunmetal concrete wall background. Realistic paper texture throughout, high-end studio lighting emphasizing the lifted edges, sharp focus on ink and paper grain. ar 4:5

How to Use

This prompt creates a complex mixed-media collage effect that relies on the AI's ability to parse both the facial identity and the grid structure. For best results in Gemini, upload a clear shoulders-up reference photo with good lighting and a neutral background. The AI will attempt to fragment and reconstruct that exact face across the 5x6 paper grid. The grid is described as 'slightly irregular,' which helps the composition feel organic rather than robotic. If the AI fails to align the facial features correctly across the tiles, you can simplify the prompt initially by removing the text overlays and sticky notes, generating the base paper fragment face first, then using inpainting or a second pass to add the handwritten quotes. You can customize the handwritten text on the face and the sticky notes to any quotes or words that fit your personal brand or aesthetic. The 'gunmetal concrete wall' provides a cool, industrial contrast to the warm paper texture, but you can change this to 'warm brick wall' or 'dark charcoal plaster' to shift the mood.

FAQ

  • The facial features don't line up correctly across the 5x6 grid. My eye is split between two tiles and looks disconnected. How can I fix this?
    Reconstructing a face perfectly across a grid is mathematically complex for AI, as it has to align the facial map with a geometric overlay simultaneously. To improve alignment, you can add more explicit spatial instructions. Try appending: `The facial features align precisely with the grid lines so that no single feature (eyes, nose, mouth) is bisected awkwardly. The eye falls entirely within a single paper tile.` This guides the AI to place the grid 'over' the face rather than cutting through the center of the pupil. If the AI still struggles, you can reduce the grid complexity from 5x6 to 3x3 or 4x4, which gives the model larger tiles to work with and less chance for misalignment.
  • The handwritten text on the face looks typed or like a computer font instead of real handwriting. How do I get that authentic ink look?
    To push the AI toward a more authentic handwriting texture, use descriptive adjectives for the writing instrument and style. Instead of just `handwritten text,` use `hasty black ballpoint pen scrawl` or `messy Sharpie marker strokes.` You can also add the term `varying ink density` or `slight ink bleed on the paper texture.` This tells the AI to simulate the pressure of a hand holding a pen. If the text is still too perfect, add the word `imperfect kerning` or `organic letterforms` to break the uniformity of a digital typeface.
  • I want to use this for a movie poster parody but I need different quotes on the sticky notes. Can I change the text freely?
    Absolutely. The quotes provided in the prompt are placeholders designed to fit a specific narrative voice. You can replace the text inside the quotation marks with any custom quotes you like. For example, you can change `'Never tell me the odds.'` to `'This is the way.'` or `'Hasta la vista, baby.'` The AI will render the new text string as handwriting. Just ensure you maintain the same punctuation and formatting structure (quotation marks around the phrase) so the AI parses it as a distinct text element. The length of the quote should ideally be similar to the original to fit comfortably on the sticky note without the AI truncating it.
