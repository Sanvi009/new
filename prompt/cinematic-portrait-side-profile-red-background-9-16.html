Frequently Asked Questions

  • What does 'rim light' mean?
    Rim light is a lighting technique where light hits the subject from behind and slightly to the side, creating a bright outline or 'rim' along the edges of the hair and shoulders. Here it's specified as red for dramatic effect.
  • Why specify '85mm lens' and 'shallow depth'?
    85mm is a classic portrait focal length that flatters facial features. Shallow depth of field blurs the background, keeping focus 100% on the face. AI models trained on photography understand these terms and replicate the look.
  • The prompt says 'gentle smile' and 'serious side look' — which one wins?
    The stronger instruction is 'serious side look profile looking left'. The smile instruction may be ignored or produce a neutral expression. For best results, remove one or the other.
  • What are 'transparent frame glasses'?
    Glasses with clear or nearly invisible plastic frames, also called 'clear glasses' or 'see-through frames'. Popular in modern professional and editorial portraits.
  • Can I get this look on a phone wallpaper?
    Yes, the 9:16 aspect ratio is designed for vertical screens. The shallow depth of field and centered composition also work well for profile pictures and lockscreens.
