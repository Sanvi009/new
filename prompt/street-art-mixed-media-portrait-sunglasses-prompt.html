Generate a gritty street-art editorial portrait from your photo. Black-and-white ink sketch with cross-hatching, translucent black sunglasses, vintage newspaper collage background, orange and blue paint splashes. 9:16.

Prompt Explanation

This prompt is designed to transform a personal photograph into a raw, energetic piece of street art, blending the subject's likeness with the aesthetic of hand-made mixed media illustration. Its creative purpose is to create an editorial-style portrait that feels ripped from a zine or a gritty urban poster, using a combination of ink sketching, collage, and paint splashes to convey a rebellious and contemporary mood. The goal is not to replicate a photograph, but to use the photo as the basis for a stylized, graphic interpretation that has texture, depth, and a handmade feel. The prompt guides the AI by first establishing the source material: the uploaded image is the primary subject reference. It then immediately sets the stylistic direction, calling for a high-impact mixed-media editorial portrait in a gritty street-art illustration style. The description of the subject is specific: a chest-up composition with the head tilted slightly upward, wearing bold translucent black sunglasses. These sunglasses are crucial as they are designated the main color accent, a clever detail because even though they are black, their translucency and sheen provide a focal point against the black-and-white sketch. The prompt then dives into the artistic techniques, instructing the AI to render the face and body in detailed black-and-white ink sketching with rough pencil lines, cross-hatching, and hand-drawn texture. This is a direct command to abandon photorealism and emulate analog drawing methods. The background is described as a layered collage of vintage newspaper clippings and typography, partially visible and distressed, which adds a sense of history and found-object art. This is blended with expressive paint splashes in vibrant orange and deep blue, scattered dynamically. The choice of orange and blue, complementary colors, is intentional to create maximum visual energy against the monochrome sketch and paper. The prompt further adds to the texture with ink splatters, paint drips, grain, and paper texture, all contributing to the raw, rebellious, contemporary poster feel. The lighting is described as high contrast and dramatic, but it's a graphic, illustrative lighting, not a photographic one. The final, and perhaps most important, set of instructions are the explicit prohibitions: no realism, no smooth skin, no photorealistic finish. These negative prompts are essential for steering the AI away from its default tendency towards smooth, photographic outputs and towards the desired hand-drawn aesthetic. The elements that must not be changed are the subject's core facial structure (as derived from the uploaded image) and the presence of the translucent black sunglasses as a key accent. The overall mixed-media, non-realistic style is also non-negotiable. The prompt variations offer numerous ways to explore different moods and aesthetics. Changing the accent color of the sunglasses to red, yellow, or green would shift the focal point's energy. Replacing the orange and blue splashes with pink and cyan would create a more pop-art or vaporwave feel. Swapping newspaper clippings for music sheets would tailor the image more directly for album art. Modifying the head tilt changes the subject's attitude, from the suggested slight uptilt to a more confrontational straight-on gaze. For practical tips, using a moderate image weight in tools like Midjourney is crucial; too high and the AI will cling to the photo's realism, too low and the likeness may be lost. The goal is to preserve the pose and the shape of the sunglasses while allowing the style transfer to fully take over. Generating multiple times is necessary to achieve a balanced composition where the collage, paint splashes, and ink sketch coalesce harmoniously without one element overwhelming the others. A common mistake is using a reference photo with overly complex lighting or shadows, which can confuse the AI when it tries to translate that into ink cross-hatching. Another is failing to include the no realism directives, which often results in a slightly stylized photo rather than a true illustration. This prompt is a masterclass in using descriptive, technique-oriented language to guide an AI away from its comfort zone and into the realm of expressive, handmade art, creating a portrait that is not just a likeness, but a piece of visual art with its own texture and story.

The Prompt 

Using my uploaded image as the primary subject reference, recreate a high-impact mixed-media editorial portrait in a gritty street-art illustration style. The subject is shown from the chest up, head tilted slightly upward, wearing bold translucent black sunglasses that act as the main color accent. Render the face and body in detailed black-and-white ink sketching with rough pencil lines, cross-hatching, and hand-drawn texture. The background should be a layered collage of vintage newspaper clippings and typography, partially visible and distressed, blended with expressive paint splashes in vibrant orange and deep blue scattered dynamically across the canvas. Add ink splatters, paint drips, grain, and paper texture for a raw, rebellious, contemporary poster feel. High contrast, dramatic lighting, sharp detail on facial features, energetic composition, urban art aesthetic, modern editorial illustration, no realism, no smooth skin, no photorealistic finish. aspect ratio 9:16

How to Use This Prompt

1. Upload your reference photo as an image prompt. 2. Copy the full prompt exactly as written. 3. Paste into Midjourney, DALL-E, or Stable Diffusion. 4. Set aspect ratio to 9:16. 5. Use moderate image weight to preserve pose and sunglasses, but allow style transfer. 6. Generate multiple times to achieve balanced collage and paint splash composition.

Why This Prompt Works

It explicitly prohibits photorealism ('no realism, no smooth skin, no photorealistic finish') which pushes the model toward hand-drawn illustration styles. It names specific analog techniques (cross-hatching, pencil lines, ink splatters, paint drips) that AI can replicate. It assigns a single color accent (translucent black sunglasses) in an otherwise black-and-white sketch, creating a focal point. Orange and blue are complementary colors that create visual energy against distressed newspaper text.

Prompt Variations

  • Change accent color sunglasses to red, yellow, or green
  • Replace orange/blue splashes with pink/cyan or yellow/black
  • Swap newspaper clippings for music sheets or book pages
  • Modify head tilt to straight-on or profile
  • Add stencil or graffiti elements to background
  • Change ink style to woodcut or screen print
  • Add torn paper edges instead of paint drips

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What does 'translucent black sunglasses' look like?
    Semi-transparent dark lenses where you can partially see the eyes beneath. Not opaque. Acts as a color accent despite being black due to sheen and reflection.
  • Will the AI include real newspaper text?
    Vintage newspaper clippings will show blurred, fragmented, or distressed lettering, not legible articles. The prompt says 'partially visible and distressed'.
  • How is this different from photorealistic portraits?
    No smooth skin, no soft gradients, no photographic lighting. Uses visible linework, cross-hatching, rough pencil texture, and paint splatters. Looks handmade.
  • What does 'energetic composition' mean?
    Dynamic placement of paint splashes, drips, and collage elements. Not symmetrical or perfectly balanced. Feels spontaneous and raw.
  • Can I use this for album art or posters?
    Yes, the prompt is designed for contemporary poster aesthetics, editorial illustration, and urban art applications.
  • Why orange and blue specifically?
    Complementary colors that create high visual contrast against black-and-white ink and distressed paper. Both vibrant without clashing.
