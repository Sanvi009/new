How to Use

This prompt creates a highly specific macro tabletop illusion. For the best results in Gemini, the AI does not require an uploaded face reference for the miniature humans, as the prompt focuses on generic 'real living humans' with realistic texture. However, if you want to place yourself or a specific person at the tiny table, you can modify the prompt to include: `One of the miniature humans has the exact same facial and physical identity as the uploaded reference image.` The key to the realism lies in the contrast between the macro scale and the detailed textures. If the food looks like plastic, add specific dish descriptions: `eating steaming spaghetti carbonara and a crisp green salad with glistening dressing.` The WhatsApp doodle background must be 'seamless' and 'edge-to-edge.' If the AI leaves a blank border, add the phrase `the pattern extends infinitely to all four edges of the frame.` The text inside the bubble is a specific social call-to-action; you can replace the string `"you should follow brainloom bro"` with any custom message you like.

FAQ

  • The miniature humans look like dolls or plastic toys. How do I make them look like real shrunken people?
    This is the most common issue with macro-scale AI generation. To push the rendering toward 'real living human' as opposed to 'action figure,' you need to emphasize organic, biological details. Add specific descriptors about movement and life: `Their tiny chests rise and fall with subtle breath,` `micro-expressions visible on their faces,` and `one is mid-gesture, hand blurred slightly with motion.` Avoid words like 'figure,' 'model,' or 'miniature' without the qualifier 'living.' Instead, use `real people photographed at 5x macro magnification.` This frames the prompt as a camera trick rather than a toy diorama.
  • The WhatsApp doodle background looks like a messy scribble. How do I get the clean, recognizable pattern?
    The AI needs a stronger visual reference for the specific WhatsApp pattern. Instead of just 'doodle pattern,' use a more precise description: `The background is entirely covered in a seamless, repeating tile pattern of simple thin line-art icons: hearts, stars, smiley faces, speech bubbles, and peace signs, in a soft grey on off-white, identical to the dense WhatsApp chat wallpaper.` Providing a list of the specific icons helps the AI construct the pattern rather than inventing random squiggles. Also, adding `highly repetitive and uniform wallpaper texture` cues the AI to treat it as a background asset rather than a drawing.
  • The text inside the speech bubble is gibberish or has spelling errors. Can I fix the typography?
    AI image generators are notoriously bad at spelling, especially in complex scenes like this macro setup. While the prompt specifies exact text, the AI may render `"you should follow brainloom bro"` as `"yuo shuld folow bralnom bro."` For a social media post where the text is the entire point, the most reliable method is to generate the image as a beautiful, empty bubble table (remove the text instruction from the prompt temporarily). Then, use a photo editing app to overlay a real WhatsApp screenshot bubble with perfect typography onto the table surface. This gives you the 8K hyper-realistic photography combined with flawless vector text rendering.
