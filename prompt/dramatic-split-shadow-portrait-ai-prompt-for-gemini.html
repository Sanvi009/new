How to Use

This prompt excels at creating high-contrast editorial portraits and relies heavily on the specific lighting direction. For best results in Gemini, upload a clear, front-facing reference photo with even lighting and no heavy shadows on the face. The AI will then apply the described 'hard-edged key light' and shadow placement. If the shadow does not slice exactly across the center of the face as intended, you can adjust the lighting cue in the prompt. For example, changing `above and slightly to my left` to `directly from the left side at eye level` will create a more pronounced split-lighting effect rather than a top-down slash. The beanie is described as covering hair and ears, so using a reference photo where the hairline is clearly visible helps the AI accurately map where the fabric should sit. You can modify the coat color by swapping `water-resistant blue coat` with `olive green parka` or `charcoal wool coat`, but maintaining the `soft suede-like highlight` description ensures the fabric texture remains rich and editorial.

FAQ

  • The shadow across my face isn't straight or looks jagged. How do I get a clean horizontal slice?
    Achieving a perfectly straight shadow line depends on the AI's interpretation of the facial topography. The nose and brow ridge naturally create dips in a shadow line. To enforce a cleaner cut, you can add geometric terms to the lighting description. Try appending `the shadow line is a hard, straight horizontal cut` or `light is flagged to create a perfect straight edge across the face.` Additionally, using a reference photo with a very neutral, straight-on expression (not smiling or frowning) provides a flatter 'canvas' for the AI to draw that shadow line across. If the nose shadow throws off the line, you can specify `shadow cuts just below the eyes, leaving the nose bridge in shadow` to adjust the vertical placement.
  • The beanie looks fake or like it's floating. How can I make it sit naturally on my head?
    This is a common issue with headwear in AI generation when the reference photo has voluminous or long hair. The AI sees the hair in the reference and tries to render the beanie over it, often resulting in a weird bulge or gap. For the best fit, use a reference image where your hair is already pulled back, slicked down, or short. If that's not possible, add the descriptor `the beanie fits snugly against the skull, compressing the hair beneath it.` This gives the AI a physical cue to flatten the volume. You can also change `fuzzy black beanie` to `thin skull cap beanie` which naturally sits closer to the head shape.
  • Can I use this prompt to get a clean, bright portrait without the shadow?
    The core identity of this specific prompt is the 'dramatic split shadow.' If you remove the hard-edged directional light description, you lose the entire mood of the image. However, you can absolutely modify the lighting to be 'softer' while keeping the exact same outfit and face preservation. To do this, replace the lighting section with: `soft, diffused Rembrandt lighting with a gentle key light from the left, creating a subtle triangle of light on the shadowed cheek.` This will give you a classic, flattering portrait instead of a harsh shadow slice. Alternatively, for a completely clean look, use `bright, even butterfly lighting with no shadows on the face.`
