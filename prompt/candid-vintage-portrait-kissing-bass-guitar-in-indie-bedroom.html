How to Use

This prompt is designed for AI image generators that support reference image uploads, such as Gemini, Midjourney, or Stable Diffusion with ControlNet. First, upload a clear self-portrait or image of the intended subject to establish the facial identity. Paste the prompt text exactly into the generation field. To tweak the output, consider changing the color of the bass guitar or swapping the graphic tee for a different band shirt to personalize the scene. You can also adjust the poster references from retro ads to specific album covers that match your taste. If the lighting appears too harsh, soften the instruction to 'soft flash photography' or add 'golden hour fill light'. For best results, ensure your reference image has good front-facing visibility of the face and use a 4:5 aspect ratio for the classic vertical portrait crop. Experimenting with the 'stylization' or 'image weight' settings will help balance the fidelity of your face with the desired film grain and nostalgic texture.

FAQ

  • How do I make sure the AI keeps my exact face in this prompt?
    The key phrase in this prompt is 'exact same facial and physical identity from the uploaded image.' This works best with multimodal models like Gemini or with Stable Diffusion workflows that support IP-Adapter or FaceID models. For the most accurate results, use a reference image that is well-lit, front-facing, and has minimal obstructions. If using Midjourney, you may need to use the '--cref' URL command followed by your image link and adjust the '--cw' parameter (character weight) to balance facial likeness with the desired artistic style. A setting around 50-75 usually maintains identity while allowing the film grain and lighting to take effect.
  • What if I don't have a poster wall, will the AI add generic images?
    The AI will generate a background based on the description 'wall filled with eclectic posters and prints.' Because the prompt includes specific notes like 'retro ads, movie posters, warning signs,' the generator will typically create a collage that looks authentic to a 90s indie bedroom. You can further control this by editing the prompt to include specific fictional band names or movie titles inside the parentheses. For example, changing it to '(including Pulp Fiction poster, Clash flyer, and vintage cigarette ads)' will guide the AI to render those specific visual elements, even if they are slightly distorted or textually vague due to the shallow depth of field.
  • Can I change the aspect ratio or will it ruin the composition?
    The prompt ends with '--ar 4:5' which is ideal for a vertical portrait that feels like a film photograph. You can safely change this to 9:16 for a smartphone wallpaper or 2:3 for a classic 35mm film look. Changing it to a wide horizontal ratio like 16:9 will alter the intimate framing, likely showing more of the messy bedroom and less of the subject's face and guitar interaction. If you need a square format for social media, use 1:1 but be aware the AI might crop the top or bottom of the poster wall. The composition will still maintain the grainy texture and lighting described, just within the new frame constraints.
