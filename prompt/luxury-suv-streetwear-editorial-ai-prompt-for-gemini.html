How to Use

This prompt is optimized for a specific low-angle editorial composition and works best in Gemini when you upload a clear, front-facing reference photo with a neutral or serious expression. The AI will map your exact facial identity onto the described pose and setting. The low-angle perspective is crucial to the 'powerful' streetwear vibe; to ensure the AI captures this correctly, you can emphasize the camera height by adding `shot from ground level looking up` if the initial result appears too eye-level. The fog and haze are subtle atmospheric elements. If the fog appears too thick and obscures the subject, reduce the description from `controlled fog` to `faint atmospheric haze.` You can easily customize the outfit colors by changing `black` to `charcoal grey` or `olive green` in the hoodie and scarf descriptions while keeping the fabric weight and silhouette the same. The `subtle dark graphic print` on the hoodie can be specified further, for example, `subtle dark tonal logo print` or `faint abstract graphic.`

FAQ

  • The low-angle perspective makes my chin or neck look strange. How can I fix this?
    Low-angle shots naturally emphasize the jaw and underside of the chin, which can sometimes distort proportions if the AI misinterprets the reference photo's angle. To soften this effect while keeping the powerful low-angle look, you can adjust the camera height cue in the prompt. Change `dominant low-angle perspective` to `moderate low-angle, shot from chest height looking slightly up.` This maintains the subject's dominance without pushing the perspective to an extreme worm's-eye view. Additionally, ensure your reference photo is not already shot from a high angle, as combining a high-angle reference with a low-angle output prompt creates conflicting geometric data for the AI.
  • The fog is covering the car too much or making the image look washed out. How do I get that subtle haze?
    Controlling atmospheric density in AI is about word choice. Terms like `controlled fog` or `light smoke` are good, but if the result is too heavy, you need to specify the spatial location and opacity. Try replacing the fog description with: `a very faint, thin layer of atmospheric haze settling only at floor level around the tires and lower bumper, not affecting the clarity of the subject's face or the hood of the car.` This grounds the fog to a specific z-plane in the image. You can also add `high clarity on subject` to ensure the AI prioritizes sharpness on the person and the SUV's reflection while keeping the haze soft in the background.
  • I don't have a photo with a 'serious expression.' Will the AI change my neutral face to look angry?
    Yes, the AI will attempt to modify the micro-expressions of your uploaded reference to match the `serious expression` cue. It will slightly lower the brows, tighten the lips, and adjust the gaze intensity. If you prefer to keep your exact natural resting expression from the reference photo, simply change `serious expression` to `neutral, unbothered expression` or `calm, focused expression.` This tells the AI to maintain the facial muscle structure of your upload while matching the confident body language. The result will be a more authentic, less 'model-acting' version of the same editorial pose.
