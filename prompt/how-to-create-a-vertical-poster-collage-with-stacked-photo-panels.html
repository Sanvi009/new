How to Create a Vertical Poster Collage with Stacked Photo Panels

AI Image Prompts 2026-02-21 Brainloom

Generate a dynamic vertical poster design featuring stacked black-and-white cinematic panels and a bold full-color foreground cutout with a thick white outline. Perfect for modern portrait collages.

Generated Images

The Prompt

A VERTICAL POSTER COLLAGE DESIGN. THE BACKGROUND CONSISTS OF FOUR STACKED HORIZONTAL ROUNDED RECTANGLES. INSIDE THESE PANELS ARE BLACK AND WHITE CINEMATIC SHOTS OF ME WITH EXACT SAME FACIAL AND PHYSICAL IDENTITY FROM THE UPLOADED IMAGE  AND SUNGLASSES IN VARIOUS POSES. SUPERIMPOSED IN THE FOREGROUND ON THE LEFT SIDE IS A FULL-COLOR, HIGH-QUALITY CUTOUT OF THE SAME MAN WEARING AN UNBUTTONED PINK MAUVE SHIRT AND SUNGLASSES, STRIKING A COOL POSE. THE FOREGROUND FIGURE HAS ATHICK WHITE STICKER-STYLE OUTLINE. AR-9:16

How to Use

Start by uploading a clear, well-lit photo of yourself. The AI needs a strong reference to replicate your features across multiple instances in the collage. This prompt is quite complex because it asks for multiple versions of you in different styles (black-and-white panels and a full-color cutout) and a specific graphic design layout. Paste the entire prompt into your AI image generator. The phrase 'me with exact same facial and physical identity' is crucial for consistency. If the faces in the black-and-white panels don't match the full-color foreground figure, you may need to increase the image weight parameter (like '--iw 2' in Midjourney) to prioritize your uploaded photo. The layout is very specific: 'four stacked horizontal rounded rectangles' as the background, with a 'full-color cutout' superimposed on the left. If the AI struggles with the stacking or the positioning, you can add clarifiers like 'the foreground figure overlaps the panels on the left edge.' The 'thick white sticker-style outline' is a key design element—if it's missing or too thin, emphasize it with terms like 'bold white border' or 'sticker effect.'

FAQ

  • What does 'thick white sticker-style outline' add to the design?
    This is a classic graphic design technique that creates separation and emphasis. It makes the foreground figure look like a physical sticker or cutout placed on top of the background, adding a tactile, pop-art, and visually striking element to the collage.
  • How can I ensure all the versions of 'me' look consistent?
    The key phrase is 'me with exact same facial and physical identity.' Using a strong, clear reference photo and potentially increasing the image weight parameter (like '--iw 2' in Midjourney) will help the AI maintain consistent features across the different panels and the foreground figure.
  • Why are the background panels described as 'cinematic shots'?
    The term 'cinematic' tells the AI to aim for a specific moody, dramatic, and high-quality aesthetic. It implies careful lighting, interesting composition, and a sense of narrative, which elevates the simple panel images from snapshots to something more artistic and cohesive.
