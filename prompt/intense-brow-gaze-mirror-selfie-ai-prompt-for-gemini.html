How to Use

This prompt captures a specific intense, brooding mirror selfie with the head tilted down and eyes looking up. For the best results in Gemini, upload a clear reference photo with neutral lighting. The AI will map your exact facial identity onto this new pose and attitude. The 'eyes looking up through the brows' is the key expression cue; if the gaze appears too soft or not intense enough, add `piercing eye contact with the reflection, slightly hooded eyelids.` The raised arm against the mirror adds a dynamic triangle to the composition. If the AI fails to render the mirror edge correctly, specify `hand pressed flat against the cool glass surface of the mirror, condensation smudge visible around fingertips.` The prompt ends with an incomplete phrase `at a slig,` which should be completed as `at a slight downward angle.` The 'tully unbuttoned' is a typo for 'fully unbuttoned.' Ensure you correct this in your generation for accurate fabric rendering. The red stains should look organic; use the material cues from the previous prompt to ensure they read as smeared theatrical blood or paint rather than skin discoloration.

FAQ

  • The 'eyes looking up through the brows' makes me look angry or villainous. How can I make it 'calm yet powerful' instead of aggressive?
    The difference between 'intense' and 'angry' lies in the relaxation of the brow and mouth. To soften the expression while keeping the powerful gaze, add specific muscle cues. Replace `intense stare` with `soft but unwavering eye contact, brows relaxed and not furrowed.` Add a cue for the mouth: `lips gently parted around the cigarette, no tension in the jaw.` You can also describe the eyes as `knowing` or `patient` rather than `piercing.` This tells the AI to keep the brow muscles neutral while the head tilt and eye direction do the work of creating presence.
  • The raised arm looks stiff and unnatural, like I'm holding an invisible object. How do I make the pose look relaxed?
    To relax the raised arm, you need to describe the weight and gravity acting on it. Instead of just `arm raised above head,` use `arm lazily raised, elbow slightly bent, wrist resting heavily against the top edge of the mirror frame.` Adding `fingers loosely curled, not splayed` prevents the hand from looking like a starfish. The 'subtly flexing forearm' is good, but it needs a reason. Add `the weight of leaning forward shifts onto the raised arm, causing a natural flex in the forearm.` This creates a chain of physical cause-and-effect that results in a more organic pose.
  • The 'fully unbuttoned white shirt' looks like a stiff costume piece. How can I get realistic, heavy fabric drape?
    Fabric realism comes down to material weight and gravity cues. `White shirt` is too generic. Specify the fabric type: `worn, slightly wrinkled white cotton poplin shirt` or `lightweight white linen shirt.` Then add gravity cues: `The open shirt hangs heavily from the shoulders, pulling the collar wide and creating deep, vertical folds of fabric that cascade down the chest. The fabric is not stiff; it clings and drapes with natural movement.` Describing how the garment interacts with the body (pulling at the shoulder seams, draping away from the skin) gives the AI the physics information it needs to render the shirt as a soft, wearable item rather than a rigid cutout.
