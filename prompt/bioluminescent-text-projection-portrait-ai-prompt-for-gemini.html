How to Use

This prompt is designed for precise identity preservation and works best when you upload a single, high-quality reference photo with even, neutral lighting. To get the exact projection effect, ensure your reference image has a relatively clear forehead and cheeks so the AI has a smooth 'canvas' for the text. When pasting this prompt into Gemini or similar image generators, pay close attention to the `3/4 view` instruction—if your reference photo is dead-on front facing, the AI may struggle to adjust the head angle. For better results, use a reference image already shot at a slight three-quarter turn. If the text projection appears too sharp or painted on rather than 'projected,' you can add emphasis by appending phrases like `the text is slightly out of focus due to depth of field` or `light bleed effect on text edges.` The prompt locks the aspect ratio to 4:5 for optimal portrait framing.

FAQ

  • How do I make the projected text look like actual handwriting instead of just random squiggles?
    AI models have improved significantly at rendering text, but they still treat it as a visual texture rather than literal words. To guide the model toward a more authentic 'handwriting' look, you can specify the language or script type in the prompt, such as `cursive English love letters` or `scientific Greek equations.` The key phrase in this prompt is `organic, imperfect handwriting style` and `text wraps naturally around cheeks.` If the AI output returns gibberish symbols that look like handwriting but aren't real words, that is actually the intended effect for this specific aesthetic. It provides the visual mood of text projection without the distraction of the viewer trying to read and parse the content. If you absolutely need legible English words, you will likely need to composite them in post-production using Photoshop's Displacement Map filter to warp real text over the 3D contours of the face.
  • The projection looks like paint on the skin instead of light. What setting should I adjust?
    This is a common issue related to the `opacity` and `blend mode` interpretation by the AI. To reinforce the 'light projection' illusion, emphasize the interaction between the light and the background darkness. You can add descriptive terms like `the projected light fades softly at the edges of the face` or `light bleed effect.` Also, ensure the prompt includes a request for a `slight blur and distortion following facial contours.` Light projected onto a 3D surface from an angle naturally distorts and falls out of focus on the edges of the face, whereas paint remains sharp. If the AI still renders it as paint, try adding the term `caustic light refraction` to the lighting description.
  • Can I change the color of the projection without ruining the face identity?
    Yes, changing the color of the projection is one of the safest modifications you can make without compromising the strict facial identity preservation. The face is lit by this light, but the underlying structure comes from your reference photo. You can replace `cyan/teal` with `amber/gold`, `crimson`, or `violet` as seen in the variations. The AI will adjust the color cast on the skin accordingly. However, be cautious when using extremely saturated red or green light, as it can sometimes wash out skin texture detail. To counter this, you can add a safeguard phrase like `skin texture remains ultra-detailed and visible despite the colored light.`
