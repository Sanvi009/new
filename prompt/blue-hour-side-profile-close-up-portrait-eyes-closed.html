Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is blue hour photography?
    Blue hour is the period after sunset or before sunrise when the sky takes on a deep blue tone. It creates soft, cool, diffused light perfect for moody portraits.
  • How do I get both blue lighting and warm key light in one image?
    The prompt balances both: cool blue light dominates the scene, while a separate warm light source gently hits the face. This mimics real lighting setups with mixed color temperatures.
  • What if I don't have freckles in real life?
    Remove 'visible freckles' from the prompt, or replace with other skin details like 'visible pores' or 'natural skin imperfections' for realism.
  • Can I use this for a front-facing portrait instead of side profile?
    Yes, replace 'close-up side profile' with 'close-up front view' or 'direct gaze' while keeping the rest of the lighting description.
  • What does 'gentle falloff' mean?
    It means the light gradually fades into shadow rather than cutting off sharply, creating a soft, natural transition on the skin.
