Prompt Explanation

This prompt is engineered to generate a specific type of extreme sports photography that blends fashion, action, and breathtaking perspective into a single cohesive image. The core concept revolves around creating a point-of-view shot that puts viewers directly into the experience of freefalling above Earth, with the subject visible in the frame as if recorded by an action camera mounted on their body or held in front of them. The prompt begins with "Ultra-cinematic 360° action-cam view," which establishes both the technical approach and the emotional tone. "Ultra-cinematic" tells the AI to aim for movie-quality visuals with dramatic lighting, rich colors, and professional composition. "360° action-cam view" specifies the perspective—this isn't a traditional photograph but rather the immersive, wide-angle perspective associated with GoPro and similar cameras. This combination immediately separates this prompt from standard portrait or landscape generations. "High above Earth, curved horizon and city far below" sets the scene with geographical context. The mention of "curved horizon" is particularly important because it establishes altitude—you need to be extremely high up to see Earth's curvature, which adds to the thrill and scale of the image. The "city far below" gives viewers a reference point for just how high the subject is, creating a sense of vertigo and excitement that makes the image compelling. The pose description is remarkably detailed because body positioning in freefall follows specific physics. The "slightly arched belly down" position is the standard stable freefall posture that skydivers use to control their descent. "Arms naturally spread for balance" continues this realistic detail, while "fingers relaxed (no stiff gestures)" addresses a common AI failure where hands look like claws or mannequin parts. By explicitly stating what not to do, the prompt reduces the chance of anatomical errors. Foreshortening instruction for the Jordan sneakers shows sophisticated understanding of perspective rendering. In wide-angle action shots, anything close to the lens appears exaggerated in size—this is a hallmark of action camera footage that adds authenticity. By specifically asking for this effect, the prompt guides the AI to create a more convincing first-person perspective rather than a flat, evenly scaled image. The outfit combines streetwear staples with custom details. The white hoodie serves as a canvas for the "FOLLOW graffiti text," which adds personality and a sense of urban culture. The red bomber jacket and red cargo pants create a bold color scheme that pops against the blue Earth and sky, ensuring the subject remains visually distinct from the background. The instruction that all clothing is "whipping in wind" adds crucial motion blur and fabric dynamics that sell the high-speed descent illusion. The final element—"focused confident smile"—provides emotional direction that transforms the image from a technical exercise into a human story. This expression tells viewers that the subject is experienced, enjoying the moment, and completely in control despite the extreme situation. It adds aspirational quality to the image, making viewers want to experience that same feeling of freedom and excitement. Common mistakes when using this prompt include not providing a strong reference image for the face, which results in generic features that don't match the intended subject. Another issue is expecting perfect results on the first generation—complex scenes with specific poses, perspective tricks, and text elements often require multiple attempts or slight prompt adjustments. Users should also be aware that different AI generators handle perspective and foreshortening with varying success; Midjourney tends to excel at dramatic landscapes and poses, while DALL-E might handle text elements more reliably. To push results further, consider adding technical photography terms like "shot on RED Komodo" or "35mm anamorphic lens" if your generator responds well to camera references. You can also experiment with lighting directions—adding "golden hour sunlight" or "sun reflecting off clouds" can add warmth and depth to the scene. The key to mastering this prompt is understanding that each element serves a specific purpose: the pose creates realism, the perspective creates immersion, the clothing creates style, and the expression creates emotional connection. When all these elements work together, the result is an image that feels like a frame from an actual extreme sports film rather than just another AI generation.

The Prompt 

Ultra-cinematic 360° action-cam view high above Earth, curved horizon and city far below. A person (use uploaded face) captured in a realistic freefall pose: body slightly arched belly down, arms naturally spread for balance, fingers relaxed (no stiff gestures), knees slightly bent, feet trailing behind with red white Jordan sneakers foreshortened in foreground. White hoodie with FOLLOW graffiti text on front, red bomber, red cargo pants whipping in wind, focused confident smile.

How to Use This Prompt

Start by uploading a clear front-facing photo of the person you want to feature in this freefall scene. The AI needs a good reference to match facial features accurately, so choose an image with even lighting and minimal shadows. Once uploaded, paste the entire prompt into your preferred AI image generator like Midjourney, DALL-E 3, or Stable Diffusion. This prompt creates a complex perspective shot, so the first generation might not get everything perfect. Pay special attention to the 360° action-cam view—this tells the AI to create a wide, immersive perspective that shows the Earth's curvature. If the horizon looks flat or the city below is unclear, try adding terms like "ultra-wide angle lens" or "fisheye effect" to strengthen the perspective. The body position requires specific attention: "slightly arched belly down, arms naturally spread for balance, fingers relaxed." These details prevent the common AI mistake of generating stiff, mannequin-like poses. If the pose looks unnatural, you can regenerate or use inpainting tools to adjust specific body parts. The Jordan sneakers should appear in the foreground with foreshortening—this means they'll look larger and slightly distorted due to the camera angle, which adds to the realistic action-cam feel. Clothing details matter for the overall aesthetic. The white hoodie with "FOLLOW" graffiti text, red bomber jacket, and red cargo pants all need to show wind movement. If the fabric looks static, add terms like "fabric rippling in wind" or "dynamic wind effect" to enhance the sense of motion. The focused confident smile on the subject's face should look natural, not forced—if expressions appear odd, regenerate with "natural smile" or "relaxed expression" added. For best results with the 360° view, consider using a 16:9 aspect ratio or even a panoramic format to capture the full sweep of Earth below. You can also experiment with adding atmospheric details like clouds, sunlight reflecting off the city, or a gradient from blue sky to dark space at the top of the frame.

Why This Prompt Works

This prompt succeeds because it combines specific technical camera directions with detailed subject positioning and fashion elements. The phrase "Ultra-cinematic 360° action-cam view" immediately establishes both the visual style and the perspective, telling the AI to think like a GoPro-style camera rather than a standard portrait lens. This distinction is crucial because it changes everything about composition, depth of field, and how elements are scaled within the frame. The prompt provides explicit anatomical instructions that prevent common AI failures. By specifying "body slightly arched belly down, arms naturally spread for balance, fingers relaxed (no stiff gestures), knees slightly bent," it gives the AI a clear physical template to work from. AI models often generate hands and limbs in awkward positions when left to their own devices, so these detailed pose descriptions dramatically improve anatomical accuracy. The parenthetical note about "no stiff gestures" is particularly effective because it tells the AI what to avoid, reducing the chance of robotic-looking hands. Including foreshortening for the Jordan sneakers shows deep understanding of perspective rendering. AI doesn't naturally understand that objects closer to the camera should appear larger and slightly distorted—it tends to render everything in proportion unless explicitly told otherwise. By specifying "foreshortened in foreground," the prompt guides the AI to create a more realistic wide-angle perspective that mimics actual action camera footage. The clothing description incorporates both specific items ("white hoodie," "red bomber," "red cargo pants") and dynamic elements ("whipping in wind"). This combination ensures the AI generates recognizable streetwear while also adding motion that sells the freefall illusion. The "FOLLOW graffiti text" on the hoodie adds a personal, styled touch that makes the image feel less generic and more like a custom piece of content. Finally, the emotional direction ("focused confident smile") gives the subject a specific expression that fits the extreme activity. Without this, AI might generate a neutral face or even a scared expression that would contradict the adventurous tone. By tying expression to the action, the prompt creates emotional coherence throughout the entire image.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why does my generated image not show the Earth's curve clearly?
    If the Earth's curve isn't prominent, try adding 'high altitude' or 'edge of space' to the prompt. You can also specify 'visible atmospheric blue halo' or 'thin blue line of atmosphere' to emphasize the curvature more dramatically.
  • How can I make the Jordan sneakers look more realistic and detailed?
    Add specific details like 'authentic Nike Air Jordan logo,' 'visible stitching,' and 'reflective red leather' to the prompt. You can also mention 'detailed shoe texture' or 'hyperrealistic sneakers' for better results.
  • The 'FOLLOW' text on the hoodie keeps coming out wrong. How do I fix this?
    Keep the text in quotation marks and consider adding 'bold graffiti font' or 'clear street art lettering.' Some AI generators handle text better if you place it earlier in the prompt or use a platform specifically trained on typography.
