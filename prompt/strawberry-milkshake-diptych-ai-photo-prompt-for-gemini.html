How to Use

To use this prompt effectively, you will need to upload two clear, high-resolution reference images of the same person to Gemini AI or a similar platform that supports face-consistency features. First, ensure your reference images have good, even lighting on the face to achieve that bright sunlight editorial look. Paste the prompt exactly as provided into the text field. If the AI struggles with placing the glass perfectly on the seam between the two images, you can add emphasis to the framing by specifying 'exact vertical split screen' or 'hard frame break.' You can tweak minor details like the flavor of the milkshake by changing 'strawberry' to 'chocolate' or 'vanilla,' but keep the color contrast in mind against the yellow and green shirts. For better results regarding the face replication, make sure the uploaded photos show the face clearly without heavy shadows or extreme angles. The prompt is optimized for a 9:16 vertical aspect ratio, so set your output dimensions accordingly to maintain the intended editorial composition and avoid cropping the building background or the interacting hands.

FAQ

  • Why does the AI sometimes change the face slightly even when the prompt says not to?
    This is a common limitation with current AI image models regarding 'face consistency' across different poses and angles within a single generation. Even with a direct instruction to use the uploaded image as an exact replica, the model interprets the scene as a whole and may make micro-adjustments to fit the new lighting, angle, or expression described (like pursed lips). To improve accuracy, ensure the face is the largest, clearest part of your reference uploads. You may also need to use an additional 'inpainting' or 'face swap' tool after generation to restore the exact original features if the likeness drifts.
  • The glass is floating between the frames instead of looking like it's held. How do I fix this?
    This illusion relies heavily on the AI understanding the exact framing boundary. If the glass appears to float in a gap, try regenerating and adjusting the prompt wording slightly. You can add clarifying terms like 'the bottom half of the cup rests exactly on the bottom edge of the top frame' or 'the seam between the two photos cuts directly through the middle of the milkshake cup.' Additionally, ensure your output aspect ratio is locked to 9:16. If the AI still creates a visible gutter, you can manually crop the images together in an external editor to achieve the perfect seamless line.
  • Can I use this prompt if the two uploaded photos are of different people?
    Yes, you can use the same prompt structure for two different people. The original prompt specifies 'two versions of the same person' to create a mirror/doppelganger effect, but the AI will follow the reference images you provide. If you upload two distinct faces, the output will feature those two distinct faces interacting. This works very well for couple portraits or best-friend editorial shots. Just make sure both reference images are well-lit and facing relatively forward for the best connection with the straw.
