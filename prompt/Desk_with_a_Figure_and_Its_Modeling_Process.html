Prompt Explanation

This prompt is designed to generate a highly specific and layered image that blends the physical and digital worlds, centered around the creation and presentation of a collectible figurine. Its creative purpose is to produce a piece of meta-art that tells a story about the design process itself, making it ideal for use in product photography, promotional material for 3D artists or toy companies, and engaging content for collector communities. The prompt_text guides the AI by establishing a clear hierarchy of subjects and their relationships. It begins by defining the primary subject: a 1/7 scale intelligent figurine. The specification of scale is crucial as it informs the AI about the proportional relationship between the figurine and its environment, ensuring that the figurine does not appear too large or too small for the computer desk it sits upon. The demand for a realistic style in a real environment pushes the AI to render materials, lighting, and shadows with a high degree of verisimilitude, avoiding a purely illustrative or cartoonish aesthetic. The round transparent acrylic base with no text is a key detail that grounds the figurine in reality, mimicking how such items are often displayed, while the instruction to omit text prevents unwanted branding or visual clutter. The most conceptually rich element is the requirement that the computer screen displays the Zbrush modeling process of the very figurine in the scene. This creates a recursive, or meta, layer to the image, inviting the viewer to contemplate the journey from digital concept to physical object. Finally, the inclusion of a BANDAI-style toy packaging box with a two-dimensional flat illusion adds another layer of commercial and artistic context, suggesting the figurine's potential life as a mass-produced item and playing with the perception of depth and flatness. The importance of composition is paramount here; the AI must intelligently arrange the figurine, screen, and box in a believable and aesthetically pleasing way on the desk. Elements that must not be changed are the core meta-narrative components: the 1/7 scale of the figurine, its placement in a real environment, the clean, no-text acrylic base, the Zbrush modeling process on the screen, and the specific BANDAI-style packaging with its flat illusion. Altering these would dismantle the prompt's unique conceptual framework. However, many elements can be safely modified to explore different creative directions. For instance, swapping the computer desk for a workshop bench would shift the context from a finished product display to a creator's workspace. Changing the 3D software from Zbrush to Maya or 3ds Max would simply update the digital tool depicted, keeping the meta-commentary intact but appealing to users of different software. Modifying the packaging style to McFarlane Toys would alter the aesthetic from a specific Japanese collectible style to a more Western, detailed, and realistic box design. To improve accuracy and visual quality, one practical tip is to be highly specific about the lighting. While the core prompt doesn't specify lighting, adding a phrase like soft, diffused daylight from a window or the cool glow of a monitor screen can dramatically enhance realism. Another tip is to describe the desk surface in more detail, such as a sleek, dark wood grain or a cluttered creative space with a stylus and sketchpad, which would add to the narrative. Common mistakes to avoid include neglecting to specify the scale, which could result in an oddly proportioned scene, and forgetting to emphasize the realistic materials, which might lead to a plastic-looking figurine that doesn't convincingly inhabit the real environment. By adhering to the prompt's core logic, one can create a compelling image that functions as both a product showcase and a piece of conceptual art, effectively communicating the creative journey from digital sculpt to packaged product. The prompt's strength lies in its ability to weave together narrative, realism, and technical detail, making it a powerful tool for generating images that resonate with artists, collectors, and tech enthusiasts alike.

The Prompt 

Create a 1 / 7 scale intelligent figurine in the picture, in a realistic style, in a real environment. The figurine is plattered on a computer desk. The figurine has a round transparent acrylic base, with no text on the base. The content on the screen is the Zbrush modeling process of this figurine. Next to the computer screen is a BANDAI-style toy packaging box with printing. The packaging features two-dimensional flat illusion.

How to Use This Prompt

To use this prompt professionally, employ it for intelligent figurines, desk decor, or 3D showcases in tech and hobbies. Preserve "1 / 7 scale intelligent figurine," "real environment," "round transparent acrylic base," and "Zbrush modeling process" for realism. Do not change the no-text base or BANDAI packaging. Optionally vary desk items; for example, adding mouse could yield setup visuals, leading to illusions, while original keeps detailed, packaged outcomes.

Why This Prompt Works

This prompt is highly effective for a realistic figurine in a real environment with meta elements like ZBrush modeling the detailed setup with packaging and computer desk adds authenticity and scale making the image ideal for product photography collector content or tech promotions that require a blend of physical and digital realism.

Prompt Variations

  • Environment: Swap “computer desk” → “a workshop bench” for a traditional model-making setting. Swap “computer desk” → “a collector’s display case” .
  • Modeling Software: Swap “Zbrush” → “Maya” or “3ds Max” for different 3D software.
  • Packaging Style: Swap “BANDAI-style” → “McFarlane Toys style” for a more realistic, detailed box style. Swap “toy packaging” → “a clear plastic clamshell package” .
  • Illusion: Swap “two-dimensional flat illusion” → “a photorealistic image of the figure” on the box.
