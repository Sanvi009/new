Generate a striking close-up portrait with dramatic split lighting—warm amber cutting across the eyes against deep teal shadows. Perfect for moody headshots, character studies, or futuristic editorial work.

Prompt Explanation

This prompt represents the pinnacle of technical specificity in AI portrait generation. It's designed for one purpose: to create an image that doesn't look AI-generated at all. The goal is a photograph so technically precise and emotionally nuanced that viewers would assume it was captured in a professional studio with years of lighting expertise. By reverse-engineering the language of photography—lens choices, aperture settings, lighting ratios, color grading decisions—this prompt bridges the gap between what AI understands and what human photographers know. At its heart, this prompt is about controlling light. The central concept is deceptively simple: a single horizontal beam of warm light cutting across the eyes while everything else falls into cool shadow. But executing that vision requires the AI to understand spatial relationships, light direction, falloff, and color theory simultaneously. The prompt provides this guidance through layered instructions. It describes the light's color ('warm golden-orange'), its shape ('beam'), its direction ('horizontal'), its placement ('across my eyes'), and its relationship to the rest of the image ('the rest of the face is enveloped in deep teal-green shadow'). This is essentially a lighting diagram expressed in words. The inclusion of skin texture details ('visible pores and subtle imperfections') is a sophisticated touch that fights against the AI's default tendency to smooth and idealize faces. Realism in AI portraiture often comes down to these micro-details—the tiny variations in skin that make a face look like it belongs to a living, breathing human rather than a digital mannequin. By explicitly asking for imperfections, the prompt tells the AI to prioritize photographic authenticity over sanitized beauty. The technical specifications at the end deserve special attention. Most users would stop at the color and mood description, but this prompt goes further by simulating an entire camera setup. The 85mm lens at f/1.8–f/2.2 tells the AI to create shallow depth of field where the eyes are tack-sharp but the ears and background soften. ISO 100–200 indicates clean, low-noise rendering. The shutter speed of 1/125s suggests a standard handheld portrait speed, which influences how the AI handles any implied motion. These aren't arbitrary numbers—they're a shorthand that tells the AI, 'This is a photograph, not digital art. Render it accordingly.' Users can experiment with this prompt by treating the technical specs as variables to adjust. Want a more intimate, compressed look? Change the lens to 135mm. Want more background context? Open the aperture to f/5.6. Want filmic grain? Raise the ISO to 800 and add 'grain texture.' Each adjustment teaches you how the AI interprets photographic language and gives you finer control over the final aesthetic. A common mistake is neglecting the reference image quality. This prompt demands facial fidelity, and the AI can only work with what you provide. A blurry, poorly lit, or distant reference photo will produce disappointing results regardless of how perfect the prompt is. Take time to upload a sharp, front-facing image where your eyes are clearly visible and your identity is unmistakable. To push results further, consider the emotional subtext of the lighting itself. Why is only the eyes illuminated? What does it mean that the rest of the face hides in shadow? This selective lighting suggests hidden thoughts, interiority, a person keeping secrets while revealing just enough through their gaze. When the AI successfully executes this vision, the result isn't just a portrait—it's a window into a character, a frozen moment of introspection that invites viewers to wonder what lies behind those amber-lit eyes in the teal darkness.

The Prompt 

Ultra-cinematic close-up portrait of me with exact same facial and physical identity from the uploaded image , face filling the frame, . my expression is calm, introspective, and mysterious. A dramatic beam of warm golden-orange light cuts horizontally across my eyes, illuminating only the eye area while the rest of the face is enveloped in deep teal-green shadow.  The contrast between warm and cool tones creates a strong cinematic, moody atmosphere. Skin texture is highly detailed and realistic, with visible pores and subtle imperfections. The lighting is soft but directional, creating smooth gradients across the face. Background is dark, minimal, and out of focus, emphasizing the glowing eyes and emotional depth. Color palette: teal shadows + amber/orange eye light. Mood: mysterious, cinematic, futuristic, introspective. Camera & Image Settings Use these settings (or similar equivalents): Camera Lens: 85mm portrait lens Aperture: f/1.8 – f/2.2 ISO: 100–200 Shutter: 1/125s Focus: eyes Lighting Main light: narrow warm orange beam across the eyes Secondary fill: soft teal ambient light from side or below High contrast ratio (9:16 or stronger) Color grading Teal shadows Warm amber highlights Slight vignette High contrast, cinematic tone curve

How to Use This Prompt

Begin by uploading a clear, high-resolution reference photo of yourself. Since this is an extreme close-up focused on the eyes, your reference image should have your eyes sharply in focus with good detail. The AI needs to understand the unique shape of your eyes, brow bone, and facial structure to maintain your identity in the final render. Copy the complete prompt into your AI image generator. The prompt is highly detailed for a reason—it's essentially giving the AI a full photography brief. Pay special attention to the lighting description: 'a dramatic beam of warm golden-orange light cuts horizontally across my eyes.' This specific instruction tells the AI exactly where to place the key light and how it should interact with your face. The result should be a horizontal stripe of warm light crossing your eyes while everything else falls into teal shadow. The technical specifications at the end aren't just decoration. Terms like '85mm portrait lens' and 'f/1.8' tell the AI to simulate a shallow depth of field, which is why the background falls softly out of focus. 'ISO 100–200' indicates clean, grain-free skin texture. If you want more filmic grain, you could adjust this upward. The 'focus: eyes' instruction reinforces that the eyes must be the sharpest element in the frame. Because this prompt asks for highly specific lighting and skin detail, you may need to generate multiple variations. Sometimes the AI places the warm beam too high or too low. Regenerate and the placement often improves. If skin texture looks too smooth, add phrases like 'hyperrealistic skin texture' or 'visible skin details' to your next attempt. The goal is that perfect balance where the eyes glow with warm light against those deep teal shadows.

Why This Prompt Works

This prompt is extraordinarily effective because it functions like a complete creative brief for a professional photoshoot. It doesn't just describe what the image should look like—it explains the lighting setup, camera settings, color grading, and emotional tone in precise, actionable terms. This level of detail transforms a vague request into a technical specification that AI models can execute with remarkable accuracy. The prompt's structure follows a logical flow from broad concept to micro-detail. It starts with the subject ('close-up portrait of me'), moves to expression ('calm, introspective, mysterious'), then dives into the signature lighting effect ('warm golden-orange beam across the eyes'). From there, it expands to color theory ('teal shadows + amber/orange'), skin texture, background treatment, and finally the exhaustive technical specs. Each layer builds on the last, creating a complete picture in the AI's latent space. The color contrast described—teal shadows with warm amber highlights—is famously effective in cinema for a reason. It's called complementary color grading, and it creates visual tension that our eyes find naturally appealing. By naming this palette explicitly, the prompt taps into thousands of film stills and professional photographs the AI has been trained on that use this exact scheme. Movies like 'Drive,' 'Blade Runner 2049,' and countless thrillers have cemented this look as shorthand for moody, cinematic, and slightly futuristic. Including specific camera settings is a power move. Most users don't realize that AI models trained on photography datasets understand terms like '85mm portrait lens' and 'f/1.8' as instructions about depth of field, compression, and bokeh quality. 'Shutter: 1/125s' suggests a standard handheld portrait speed, which influences how the AI renders motion (or lack thereof). These details collectively signal 'professional photography' to the model, elevating the output from generic digital art to something that feels captured by a real camera with intentional technique.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why does the prompt specify both warm and cool lighting colors?
    The warm amber on the eyes and cool teal in the shadows create complementary color contrast, which makes the image visually striking and emotionally resonant. This teal-and-orange combination is a staple of cinematic color grading because it makes skin tones pop against moody backgrounds.
  • How can I make sure the AI captures my exact eye shape and features?
    Your reference photo is crucial here. Upload a well-lit image where your eyes are clearly visible and in focus. The more detail the AI has about your unique eye shape, iris color, and brow structure, the better it can maintain your identity in the final close-up portrait.
  • What if the warm light beam doesn't land exactly across my eyes?
    This is a common challenge with precise lighting prompts. Simply regenerate the image multiple times—the AI will vary the placement. You can also add directional terms like 'light coming from the left side' or 'light hitting the eyes at eye level' to give more guidance.
