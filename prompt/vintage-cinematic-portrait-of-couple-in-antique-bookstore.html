How to Use

This prompt works best with AI image generators that support multiple reference image uploads for consistent character identity, such as Gemini or Stable Diffusion with IP-Adapter FaceID. First upload clear front-facing images of both the woman and man you want featured. Paste the full prompt text into the generation field exactly as written. To customize the look, you can adjust the clothing descriptions. For instance, change 'soft beige lace blouse' to 'deep burgundy velvet top' or swap 'dark simple outfit' to 'charcoal knit sweater' to match different seasons. The bookstore setting can be refined by adding specific details like 'stacks of leather-bound classics' or 'vintage maps spread across the table.' If the film grain and scratches feel too strong, you can soften the effect by adding 'subtle grain only' or removing 'dust and scratches' from the prompt. For best results with identity preservation, ensure both reference images are well-lit headshots without harsh shadows covering the face.

FAQ

  • How do I ensure both faces look exactly like the reference images I upload?
    This prompt is designed for multi-subject identity preservation. When using Gemini or similar multimodal models, upload both reference images alongside the prompt text. The phrase 'from the uploaded image' signals the AI to map facial features directly from those files. For optimal results, use reference photos where both people are facing forward with neutral expressions and even lighting. If using Stable Diffusion with ControlNet or IP-Adapter FaceID, you will need to load separate face models for each subject. Some platforms allow you to assign specific reference images to specific text prompts using regional prompting or inpainting masks. If you notice blending of features between the two subjects, increase the weight on the 'exact facial identity' portion of the prompt or adjust the denoising strength slightly lower to preserve source details.
  • Can I use this prompt if I only have a photo of one person?
    Yes, but you will need to modify the prompt to remove references to the second person. Delete the phrase 'beside her a man ( from the uploaded image ) both of them smiling naturally at the camera' and the clothing description for the man. You can replace it with a solo description such as 'sitting alone at a table filled with books.' The AI will then generate a solo vintage cinematic portrait of the single uploaded subject. Alternatively, you can keep the man in the prompt and let the AI generate a fictional partner based on the description of a 'man in a dark simple outfit,' though the face will be a generic AI-generated likeness rather than a specific person.
  • What settings should I use to get that dreamy film grain and dust effect?
    The prompt already includes specific terms like 'film grain,' 'dust and scratches,' 'low contrast,' and 'slightly desaturated colors' to guide the AI toward an analog photography look. In Midjourney, using a lower '--stylize' value (between 50-250) often yields more natural, less digitally perfect results that match the analog aesthetic. In Stable Diffusion, using checkpoints trained on vintage film photography, such as 'Analog Diffusion' or 'Realistic Vision' with a LoRA for 'Film Grain,' will enhance this effect. If the output looks too clean, you can add 'heavy film grain' or 'scratched negative texture' to the prompt. You can also adjust the 'sharpness' or 'detail' settings in your AI tool slightly downward to mimic the softer focus of older camera lenses.
