CCTV Style Security Footage AI Prompt with Identity Preservation

AI Image Prompts 2026-04-22 Brainloom

This prompt generates a realistic CCTV-style security camera still or short clip featuring the exact facial identity of the user uploaded in a reference image. The scene places the subject in an indoor hallway or lobby, walking naturally past a high-angle surveillance camera. The subject looks up directly at the lens with a bright, friendly smile while casually waving, creating a candid and confident appearance. The visual aesthetic strictly mimics authentic security footage with specific technical artifacts: a slightly distorted wide-angle lens perspective, low resolution output, desaturated or monochrome color grading, and a visible timestamp overlay in the corner. The prompt also includes subtle scan lines, digital compression artifacts, motion blur on the waving hand, and grainy noise to ensure the result does not look like a polished photograph but rather an unpolished, documentary-style security still. The lighting is described as uneven with harsh overhead shadows, reinforcing the utilitarian and unglamorous nature of real surveillance systems while strictly preserving the user's natural facial geometry and body type.

How to Use

To get the best results with this prompt, begin by uploading a clear reference photo of your face to the AI image generator before pasting the text. This ensures the model adheres to your exact identity rather than generating a generic person. The prompt is designed for a vertical 9:16 aspect ratio, perfect for phone screens or social media stories. If you want to adjust the environment, you can swap 'hallway or lobby' with 'parking garage,' 'elevator bank,' or 'convenience store aisle' to change the mood while keeping the security aesthetic. For a different era, you might add 'VHS tape degradation' or '2000s CCTV grain' to the prompt. If the AI makes the image look too sharp or clean, emphasize 'standard definition video' or '480p resolution' in your variations. Avoid using terms like 'portrait mode' or 'soft focus' as they contradict the harsh, documentary nature of surveillance footage. The timestamp overlay is usually generated automatically by most models when 'CCTV footage' is specified, but if it doesn't appear, you can add a specific line like 'white digital timestamp in top right corner reading 04-22-2026 14:37:52.'

FAQ

  • How can I make the generated image actually look like a video still and not just a blurry photo?
    To push the result from 'blurry photo' into genuine 'video still' territory, you need to emphasize temporal artifacts in the prompt. The key is 'motion blur' and 'scan lines.' In your prompt, make sure the subject is described with a verb that implies movement, like 'walking,' 'turning head,' or 'waving hand.' Additionally, adding phrases like 'interlaced video lines' or 'frame tearing at the edge of the arm' can trick the AI into rendering a freeze-frame effect. If the result still looks too static, try specifying a slightly longer 'shutter speed' in the prompt description, such as 'slow security camera shutter creating smeared light trails on movement.' This forces the rendering engine to simulate the time-based exposure of a real security camera sensor rather than a still photography camera.
  • Why does the prompt include 'slightly confident model-like appearance' if it's supposed to be surveillance footage?
    This is a deliberate creative contrast to prevent the AI from generating a slouched, unflattering, or suspicious-looking figure. Without this modifier, AI models often associate 'CCTV footage' with 'criminal suspect' or 'blurry pedestrian,' which can result in the subject looking hunched, obscured, or deliberately hiding their face. By specifying 'slightly confident model-like appearance,' you are guiding the AI to keep the subject's posture upright and the face clearly visible and pleasant, while the surrounding environment and technical filters handle the gritty, unpolished security aesthetic. It ensures the subject is the clear focal point of the frame, just captured on a low-fidelity system.
  • Can I change the timestamp to a specific date or add custom text on the screen?
    Yes, and this is one of the most customizable parts of this prompt style. In the text, you can replace 'timestamp overlay' with a specific instruction like 'white monospace text reading 2026-04-22 09:41:03 PM CAM 04.' Most advanced AI image generators are now quite good at rendering legible, blocky alphanumeric characters when they are explicitly described. For best results, put the desired text in quotes within the prompt. You can also add location data: 'overlay text reading LOBBY NW CORNER.' Be aware that if the resolution is set very low, the text may generate as illegible squiggles. If this happens, add the keyword 'legible LCD font' to force the model to sharpen the text generation in that specific area of the image.
