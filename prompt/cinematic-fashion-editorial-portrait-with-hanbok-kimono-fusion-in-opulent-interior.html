Generate a high-end Vogue-worthy fashion portrait that places you in an opulent interior wearing a modernized hanbok and kimono fusion robe, using cinematic lighting and ultra-detailed textures.

Prompt Explanation

This prompt is designed to bridge the gap between personal identity and high-fashion fantasy. It starts with a deeply personal request—'of me'—and then layers on incredibly specific fashion and environmental details. The core idea is to allow someone who is not a professional model to see themselves transformed into a Vogue-worthy editorial subject. This taps into a powerful desire for self-expression and aspirational identity that many people have but few can afford in real life. A custom hanbok and kimono fusion garment, a private photo shoot in an opulent interior, and professional lighting setups are out of reach for most, but AI makes it accessible. The structure of the prompt is carefully engineered to guide the AI through a hierarchy of importance. First, it establishes the subject: you. By explicitly stating 'exact same facial and physical appearance from the uploaded image,' it tells the AI to prioritize the reference photo above all else. This is crucial because AI models, left to their own devices, tend to default to creating conventionally attractive but generic faces. Without this instruction, you might get a stunning portrait of someone who looks nothing like you. Next, it builds the outfit layer by layer. Starting with the color 'dark forest-green,' then the texture 'textured layered robe,' and finally the specific design elements: 'high collar, wide sleeves, asymmetrical wrap design inspired by modernized hanbok and Japanese kimono fusion.' This sequential building helps the AI construct a coherent garment. If you simply said 'wear a fancy robe,' you would get something generic and probably uninteresting. By referencing specific cultural garments like hanbok and kimono, you tap into the AI's training data, which includes thousands of images of these traditional clothes. Adding 'modernized' and 'fusion' tells it to blend these elements with contemporary fashion, resulting in something unique yet grounded in recognizable styles. The setting and lighting are described with the language of cinematography and photography. Words like 'cinematic lighting,' 'god rays,' 'high contrast shadows,' and 'shallow depth of field' are technical terms that have strong visual correlates in the AI's training data. They act as shortcuts, telling the AI to replicate the look of professional film and photography. The inclusion of specific camera equipment like 'Arri Alexa 65' and '50mm lens' further reinforces this. The AI has been trained on countless images tagged with these terms and knows they are associated with high production value. Word choice throughout the prompt is deliberate. 'Ultra-luxurious,' 'opulent,' 'vibrant,' 'dramatic,' and 'masterpiece' are all positive, high-intensity adjectives that push the AI toward generating something impressive and visually striking. They set a high bar for quality. Conversely, the prompt avoids negative words like 'no blur' or 'not ugly,' which can sometimes confuse AI and produce the opposite result. It focuses entirely on what should be present, not what should be avoided. Users can tweak this prompt in countless ways. Changing the color palette from forest-green and teal-turquoise to, say, ruby red and gold will completely shift the mood. Replacing the interior setting with an outdoor landscape changes the entire context. The pose and expression can be modified by adding phrases like 'looking away thoughtfully' or 'with a confident smirk.' Each small change ripples through the generation, producing a uniquely different result. Common mistakes include using a low-quality reference image, forgetting to include the aspect ratio and ending up with a square image, or not specifying the 'shallow depth of field' and getting a busy background that competes with the subject. For improving results further, consider using face-swapping tools after generation if the AI struggles with accuracy. Some platforms allow you to generate the scene first, then swap your face onto the model. Alternatively, you can use inpainting to refine specific areas like the hands or the folds of the robe. The key is to see the AI as a collaborator—it provides the raw material, and you refine it to match your vision.

The Prompt

How to Use This Prompt

To get the best results from this prompt, start by uploading a clear, well-lit photo of yourself. The AI needs a high-quality reference image to accurately capture your facial features and body structure. Make sure the original image shows your face clearly and is not blurry or heavily filtered. Once you have uploaded your photo, copy the entire prompt exactly as written and paste it into your AI image generator. Most platforms like Midjourney, DALL-E, or Stable Diffusion have a feature that allows you to use a reference image alongside a text prompt. Look for options like 'image prompt', 'reference image', or simply paste the image URL first. After submitting, the AI will begin generating your portrait. This prompt is quite detailed, so it may take a little longer than simpler prompts. Be patient and let it process fully. If the first result isn't perfect, don't worry. You can generate multiple variations by clicking the re-roll or generate again button. Sometimes the AI needs a few tries to get the lighting and facial details exactly right. If the hands or facial features look slightly off, you can use inpainting or editing tools to refine those areas. Many AI platforms now include editing features that let you select a specific area and regenerate it. Focus on the face first to ensure it matches your appearance, then check the clothing details and lighting. The prompt specifies a 'shallow depth of field', so the background should be slightly blurred, drawing attention to you. If the background is too sharp, you can mention 'more background blur' in a follow-up edit.

Why This Prompt Works

This prompt is highly effective because it follows a logical structure that AI image generators understand well. It starts with the subject, 'Cinematic fashion editorial portrait, of me', which immediately tells the AI what the main focus should be. By including the phrase 'exact same facial and physical appearance from the uploaded image', it signals that the reference photo is crucial and should be prioritized. The description of the outfit is extremely detailed: 'dark forest-green textured layered robe', 'high collar', 'wide sleeves', 'asymmetrical wrap design inspired by modernized hanbok and Japanese kimono fusion'. This level of specificity guides the AI to generate a very particular garment rather than a generic robe. It combines cultural references with modern fashion terms, which helps the AI pull from its training data on both traditional Asian clothing and contemporary high fashion. The setting is described with equal care: 'ultra-luxurious opulent interior', 'massive glass display cases', 'vibrant red flowers', 'bonsai trees'. This creates a rich visual environment that complements the high-fashion subject. The lighting instructions are perhaps the most important part. By specifying 'dramatic warm golden-orange cinematic lighting from above mixing with cool emerald-green ambient glow', you are telling the AI to create complex, professional lighting that mimics real fashion photography setups. Words like 'god rays', 'high contrast shadows', and 'moody cinematic atmosphere' are all terms associated with high-end visual media, and the AI recognizes these as quality indicators. Finally, the technical specs at the end—'shot on Arri Alexa 65, 50mm lens, shallow depth of field, 8k resolution'—act as a quality filter. The AI associates these terms with professional photography and will attempt to match that aesthetic. The aspect ratio 'ar-9:16' ensures the output is vertical, perfect for mobile viewing or social media stories, which is where fashion editorial content often appears today.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why does the AI sometimes change my facial features?
    AI image generators often struggle with maintaining exact facial features, especially if your reference photo is low quality or if your face is partially obscured. Using a clear, front-facing photo with good lighting gives the best chance of accuracy.
  • Can I use this prompt without uploading my own photo?
    Yes, but the result will be a generic person rather than you. The phrase 'of me' and the reference to an uploaded image are instructions to use your photo. If you remove those, the AI will create an imaginary model instead.
  • What does 'ar-9:16' mean at the end of the prompt?
    It stands for aspect ratio 9:16, which means the image will be taller than it is wide. This is the standard ratio for mobile phone screens and vertical videos, perfect for social media platforms like Instagram Stories or TikTok.
