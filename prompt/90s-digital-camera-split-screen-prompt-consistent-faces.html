Prompt Explanation

This prompt is an extraordinarily detailed and technically specific instruction set designed to generate a triptych of images that perfectly capture the raw, unpolished, and nostalgic aesthetic of late 1990s and early 2000s digital photography. Its creative purpose is to recreate the feeling of finding a memory card full of old, imperfect snapshots from a night out with friends, complete with all the charming flaws of that era's technology: the harsh flash, the red-eye, the digital noise, and the slightly off-color skin tones. The prompt achieves this by constructing a three-panel vertical strip, each panel depicting a different classic pose from that time—a cool runway-style shot, a distorted macro selfie, and an affectionate full-body portrait—all while using a reference photo to ensure the subjects' faces remain 100% consistent across all three scenes, mimicking how the same two people would appear in a real photo booth strip or a roll of film. The prompt text is a masterful blend of narrative direction and technical specification. It begins with an ironclad rule: the faces must remain identical to the reference, establishing that the subjects' identity is the anchor of the entire piece. It then meticulously scripts each of the three panels. The top panel is a wide shot with a cool fit vibe, specifying poses, expressions, and even a hidden easter egg—the number 69 subtly blended into the background. The middle panel is an extreme close-up macro selfie, instructing the AI to create goofy expressions, distorted noses, and overexposed foreheads, all hallmarks of the point-and-shoot macro mode of the era. The bottom panel is a full-body shot with affectionate posing and a background of worn band posters. This level of narrative detail ensures each panel tells a distinct part of a larger story. Crucially, the prompt then layers on a dense set of visual specifications, invoking specific camera models like the Sony Cyber-shot and Canon PowerShot A400 and technical terms like CCD sensor look, direct flash, blooming highlights, and high contrast JPEG. This lexicon is essential because it provides the AI with a clear stylistic target, moving it away from generic photography and toward a very specific, imperfect, and lo-fi digital quality. The extensive list of negative prompts, or things to avoid, is just as important. By explicitly banning smooth skin, beauty filters, studio lighting, and any form of retouching, the prompt actively fights against the AI's default tendency to beautify and perfect, forcing it to embrace the trashy Y2K vibe and the raw, unpolished look that defines the aesthetic. The insistence on avoiding CGI, 3D renders, and hyper-realistic styles ensures the final output maintains the illusion of being a real, flawed photograph rather than a digital illustration. The core, unchangeable elements are the requirement for 100% identical faces from the reference and the foundational 9:16 vertical split-screen composition with no borders. The specific technical camera aesthetic—the CCD sensor look, harsh flash, and low-resolution feel—is also sacrosanct to the prompt's identity. However, there is significant room for creative variation. The background can be changed from a messy bedroom to a mall food court or an amusement park, shifting the location of the memory without altering its era. The band posters can be replaced with video game or movie posters to reflect different interests. The lighting could be tweaked to a disposable camera flash or club lighting, both of which would enhance the nightlife feel. Even the expressions in each panel can be swapped to create a completely different narrative mood. For practical tips, users should provide a clear, front-facing reference photo for the best face consistency. Using an AI generator with strong image reference features, like Midjourney with the --cref parameter, is highly recommended. To avoid the image looking too modern and clean, it is absolutely vital to include the full list of negative prompts; omitting them is the most common mistake, as the AI will default to its training and produce a polished, filtered image. Another common error is not specifying the seamless, borderless layout, which can result in unwanted lines or padding between the panels. Ultimately, this prompt is a brilliant example of how to use highly specific, era-defining terminology and narrative scripting to guide an AI toward generating an image that feels less like a generated artifact and more like a genuine, cherished, and imperfect memory from a specific moment in technological and cultural history.

The Prompt 

Use the reference photo as the face guide. The face must remain 100 percent identical to the reference. Do not change facial structure, proportions, base expression, or any facial details.
Vertical split screen composition. Three images stacked vertically from top to bottom. Seamless edge to edge layout. No borders. No padding. Aspect ratio 9:16.
Top panel. Wide shot. Female subject and male subject posing like runway models. Wearing the exact same outfits as in the uploaded photo. The female subject crosses her arms with a stoic expression. The male subject is smoking, with visible smoke coming out. Cool fit vibe. Hard direct on camera flash. Red eye effect visible. In the background, there is a small detail of the number 69 naturally blended into the scene, such as within wall texture or room objects. Subtle and not noticeable.
Middle panel. Extreme close up macro mode selfie. The male subject wears black sunglasses with a slight smile. The female subject laughs excitedly. Noses appear distorted. Goofy expressions. Heads pressed together. Forehead appears overexposed due to flash. The number 69 is subtly hidden within light reflections or digital noise. Difficult to notice.
Bottom panel. Full body shot. The male subject stands casually with both hands on his hips. The female subject stands beside him, holding the male subject’s arm with both hands in an affectionate and excited way. Posters of well known bands are attached to the wall and appear worn. One of the posters includes a small number 69 as part of its design. Feels natural. Chaotic and playful atmosphere.
Visual specifications. Sony Cyber shot and Canon PowerShot A400 aesthetic. CCD sensor look. Early 1990s digital camera style. Direct on camera flash. Harsh shadows. Low resolution. Visible digital noise. High contrast JPEG. Oversharpened look. Slightly red skin tones. Blooming highlights. Messy bedroom background. Trashy Y2K vibe.
Avoid semi realism. Avoid CGI and 3D render. Avoid smooth skin and beauty filters. No doll like or plastic appearance. Avoid perfect symmetry. Do not use studio lighting or magazine editorial style. No retouching. Avoid soft glam. No perfect skin. Not stock photography. Avoid hyper realistic style, digital art, HDR, or render engines. Avoid bad anatomy. No extra fingers or fused limbs. No floating objects. No facial distortion that alters identity. Avoid overly clean composition.
Do not use black or white bars. No borders, panel lines, grid lines, or comic style separators.

How to Use This Prompt

1. Upload a reference photo of your subject's face to your AI image generator. 2. Copy and paste this prompt. 3. The AI will create three different photos stacked vertically while keeping the face identical. 4. Use in Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, or DALL·E with reference image features enabled.

Why This Prompt Works

This prompt uses specific technical photography terms (CCD sensor, direct flash, blooming highlights) that AI recognizes as distinct visual styles. The detailed scene descriptions help maintain consistent facial features across different poses by providing clear context for each panel.

Prompt Variations

  • Change 'messy bedroom' to 'mall food court' or 'amusement park' for different Y2K backgrounds
  • Replace 'band posters' with 'video game posters' or 'movie posters'
  • Try 'polaroid camera aesthetic' instead of 'digital camera' for film grain
  • Change lighting to 'disposable camera flash' or 'club lighting'
  • Swap 'stoic expression' for 'smiling' or 'laughing' in different panels

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do I keep the face the same in all three AI photos?
    Use the reference image upload feature in your AI generator, then use this prompt which specifically instructs the AI to maintain 100% identical facial features.
  • What AI tools work best for this 90s camera look?
    Midjourney with style raw parameter, Stable Diffusion with appropriate LoRAs for vintage cameras, or DALL·E 3 with detailed prompting all work well.
  • Why does my AI image look too modern and clean?
    Make sure to include all the 'avoid' instructions from the prompt, especially avoiding beauty filters, smooth skin, and studio lighting.
