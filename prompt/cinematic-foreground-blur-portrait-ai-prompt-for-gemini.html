Cinematic Foreground Blur Portrait AI Prompt for Gemini

How to Use

This prompt is designed to create a deep, layered image with foreground elements obscuring part of the frame. For the best results in Gemini, upload a reference photo where your face is clearly visible in a neutral or three-quarter view. The prompt specifies a side profile gaze, so the AI will rotate the head position based on your reference. If the foreground blur is not pronounced enough or missing, add weight to the composition by placing `extreme foreground bokeh` or `obscured by out-of-focus furniture` at the very beginning of the prompt. You can easily customize the cozy feel by changing the sweater description from `light-colored knitted sweater` to `chunky cable-knit cream sweater` or `cashmere V-neck` to adjust the texture. The over-ear headphones are a key prop; if you want a different accessory, swap `over-ear headphones` with `wired earbuds with a visible cable` but keep the `matte black` descriptor for that minimal cinematic look. Ensure the output aspect ratio is locked to 4:5 to maintain the intended portrait framing.

FAQ

  • The foreground blur is covering my face. How do I make the framing more open?
    This is a fine balance in the prompt interpretation. The goal is a 'voyeuristic' peek, not an obstruction of the main subject. If the AI places a big blurry blob directly over the eyes, you need to refine the spatial cues. Add specific placement words like `foreground blur at the very bottom edge of the frame` or `blurred object frames the left side of the composition only.` You can also change the object itself to something thinner, like `blurred window mullion` or `edge of a curtain.` This gives the AI a clear instruction that the blur is a framing device on the periphery, not a central mask.
  • The headphones look too bulky or like gaming headphones. How do I get a sleeker look?
    AI models often associate 'headphones' with chunky, brightly colored gaming sets. To enforce the minimalist, cinematic look described in the prompt, add specific brand-agnostic descriptors. Instead of just `over-ear headphones,` use `slim profile studio monitor headphones` or `matte black reference headphones with thin metal headband.` Adding the word `minimalist` or `audiophile` helps the AI select a more refined shape. If the ear cups still look too large, you can add `compact on-ear pads` to reduce the visual mass on the side of the head.
  • I love the warm lighting, but my skin looks too red or blotchy in the generated image. Can I fix the color cast?
    Tungsten lighting naturally has a very warm, amber-orange color temperature that can sometimes exacerbate redness in skin tones during AI generation. To preserve the moody warmth while cooling down the skin just slightly, you can add a color correction cue to the prompt: `Skin tones remain neutral and natural despite the warm ambient glow.` Alternatively, you can specify the exact light source to have a more flattering warmth, like `golden hour sunlight` instead of `tungsten lamp.` If the issue persists, you can ask for `slight desaturation in the red channel` in the color grading section, which is a technical filmmaking term that tells the AI to keep the golds but pull back the pinks.
