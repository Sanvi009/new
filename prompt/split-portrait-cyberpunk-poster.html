"Split Portrait Cyberpunk Poster Prompt" | Brainloom

Poster Design
Published: 2026-02-17
Author: Brainloom

Create a dramatic cyberpunk poster featuring your exact likeness in a split composition. One half is a sharp side-profile portrait with neon reflections, the other blends into a textured halftone dot pattern with teal geometric shapes.

Generated Images

Prompt Explanation

This prompt is designed to generate a striking, high-concept cyberpunk poster that merges photorealism with graphic design elements, using an uploaded image of a person to ensure their exact likeness is featured. The creative purpose is to produce a piece that feels both like a futuristic portrait and an editorial magazine cover, blending the personal with the abstract. The split composition is the core artistic device; it visually separates the tangible human element from the digital, patterned world of the cyberpunk genre. This duality suggests a theme of identity in a technological age, where the self is both distinct and dissolving into the data stream. The prompt is meticulously crafted to guide an AI image generator through a complex series of visual instructions. It begins by anchoring the output in reality, demanding the exact facial and physical identity from a reference image, which is non-negotiable for personalization. It then specifies a side profile facing left, a specific angle that adds a classic, almost cameo-like feel to the poster design. The inclusion of modern reflective sunglasses with vibrant red-orange neon reflections is a masterstroke, as it introduces the cyberpunk color palette directly onto the subject, making them an active participant in the scene's lighting rather than a passive element. The lighting description is extraordinarily precise, calling for dramatic cinematic lighting with strong red rim light on the left side and teal-blue on the right. This isn't just about illumination; it's about creating a high-contrast neon color grade that defines the two halves of the composition. The red light aligns with the clean portrait side, while the teal associates with the abstract, halftone-blended side, reinforcing the split thematically and visually. The prompt's explanation of the vertical split is perfect: the left half remains a clean, sharp portrait, while the right half blends into a textured halftone dot pattern that merges with a teal geometric background. This transition is the prompt's most brilliant and technically challenging aspect, instructing the AI to seamlessly integrate a photographic element with a graphic one. The background of abstract concentric circular patterns with a metallic teal gradient adds depth and a sense of technological infinity, moving beyond a simple flat color. The subject's clothing is not an afterthought; the stylish jacket must have visible fabric texture and be shaped by dramatic shadows, grounding the figure in a tangible reality even as the background becomes abstract. The skin description is also nuanced, asking for smooth retouching that retains realistic texture, preventing the subject from looking like a wax figure. Crucially, the prompt carves out typography space at the top right for a bold white title, specifically the placeholder HOMBRE, transforming the image from a simple portrait into a functional poster design layout. The elements that must not be changed are the subject's identity, the core split composition, and the specific color pairing of red and teal lighting, as these form the conceptual foundation. The gender specification and the instruction to do according to my gender ensures the generated image aligns with the user's identity. The halftone pattern could be swapped for a glitch or pixelation effect, as noted in the prompt variations, which would shift the aesthetic from retro-print to digital decay. Changing the color scheme to purple and green would alter the mood from fiery and cool to something more mystical or alien. Replacing the concentric circles with a cityscape would ground the abstraction in a recognizable cyberpunk trope, while a data stream pattern would emphasize the digital information aspect. For practical tips, uploading a clear reference photo with a visible side profile is paramount for accuracy. Using the --ar 9:16 parameter is non-negotiable for the vertical poster format. Running multiple generations is often necessary because achieving the perfect blend in the split composition can be challenging for AI; slight variations in the prompt or seed value can dramatically improve the result. A common mistake is using a low-quality or poorly lit reference image, which forces the AI to invent details, compromising the likeness. Another is neglecting to specify the gender, which can lead to a mismatch. Users also sometimes forget that the typography space is for a title; expecting the AI to perfectly render legible, custom text is a misunderstanding of current AI image generator capabilities—the space is provided for the user to add text in post-production. This prompt effectively bridges the gap between a photographic portrait and graphic poster art, leveraging the AI's ability to understand complex, multi-layered instructions to create a visually cohesive and conceptually rich final image that is both personal and professionally styled for a cyberpunk aesthetic.

How to Use This Prompt

1. Upload a clear reference photo showing the person's side profile if possible, or a front-facing shot that the AI can adapt. 2. Copy the full prompt into your AI image generator (Midjourney, DALL-E 3, Stable Diffusion). 3. Use the --ar 9:16 parameter for the correct vertical poster format. 4. Generate the image. You may need to run a few variations to get the split composition and text placement exactly right.

Why This Prompt Works

This prompt works because it describes a complex, visually interesting composition (vertical split with halftone blend) in clear terms. It pairs specific cyberpunk color theory (red rim light vs teal-blue fill) with graphic design elements (concentric circles, typography space) and photography details (side profile, reflective sunglasses). This multi-layered approach gives the AI all the information needed to produce a professional, print-ready poster design.

Prompt Variations

  • Change the split to horizontal instead of vertical.
  • Swap the halftone pattern for a glitch effect or pixelation.
  • Change the color scheme to purple and green or orange and blue.
  • Replace the concentric circles with a cityscape or data stream pattern.
  • Use a different word or name in the typography space.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is a halftone dot pattern?
    A halftone pattern uses dots of varying sizes and spacing to create gradients and shading, mimicking the look of old-school comic book printing or vintage screen printing. It adds a unique graphic texture.
  • What does 'vertically split down the center' achieve?
    It creates a dynamic, dual-reality effect. One side is a clean, realistic portrait, while the other side is graphic and abstract. This contrast is visually striking and gives a modern, editorial feel.
  • What is the 'HOMBRE' text for?
    'Hombre' is the Spanish word for 'man.' In this prompt, it serves as a placeholder title for the poster, contributing to the overall design layout. You can replace it with any word you like.
  • How do I get the side profile to match my face?
    Using a reference photo that is already a side profile will give the best results. If you only have a front-facing photo, the AI will attempt to generate a profile view based on that, but accuracy may vary. You can try using inpainting or face-swapping tools for finer control.
