The War That Lasted Less Than 40 Minutes

A fascinating piece of historical trivia

When we think of wars, we imagine long battles, years of fighting, and endless struggles. But history has one bizarre record: the shortest war ever fought lasted just 38 minutes.

It happened on 27 August 1896 in Zanzibar, an island off the coast of East Africa. At that time, Zanzibar was under strong British influence. When the Sultan of Zanzibar, Hamad bin Thuwaini, died suddenly, his cousin Khalid bin Barghash took over the palace without British approval.

The British had their own candidate in mind and demanded Khalid step down. He refused, gathering around 3,000 supporters and barricading himself inside the palace. By morning, British warships had lined up in the harbor. At 9:02 a.m., the ultimatum expired. The British opened fire.

The palace was bombarded, ships in the harbor were sunk, and the Sultan's forces collapsed almost instantly. By 9:40 a.m., Khalid had fled, and the war was over—a grand total of 38 minutes.

The result was exactly what the British wanted: their chosen Sultan was installed, and the episode went down in history as the Anglo-Zanzibar War—a conflict shorter than most people's morning commute.

It's wild to think that something as serious as a war could be over in the time it takes to watch a TV episode.
